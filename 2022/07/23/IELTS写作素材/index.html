International travel

词汇：

语句：

Cross-border travel will probably not recover to per-covid levels until 2024.

The start of the pandemic was characterized by empty supermarket shelves, as global supply chains creaked under the strain of panic buying and the disruption caused by covid-19.

But one shortage that has not been alleviated is that of international travellers.

But the prospects for 2022 look less gloomy.

Planes are still often half-full at best and many of the world’s airports remain sparsely populated.

More people will rediscover the pleasures of jumping on a plane to go on a spontaneous city break, attend a long-planned family wedding or take the holiday of a lifetime.

In the decades before the pandemic,international travel grew rapidly, with the number of visitors to foreign countries tripling between 1990 and 2019.（with+名词+分词句式，可用于小作文）

Budget airlines,growing prosperity and more leisure time underpinned this growth. These forces will eventually reassert themselves.

Restrictions on international jaunts are still tight and are lifting only slowly.

vaccination rates climb and infections fall (接种率上升且传染率下降)

Long-haul travel will remain at low levels until vaccinations are more widespread and the plethora of rules and regulations become easier to navigate.

Many meetings and conferences will remain virtual,or at least take place in hybrid form with far fewer people attending in person.

Even if a more virulent mutation of the virus emerges,potentially putting everything into reverse again,(分词结构，做前面的结果)one type of globetrotter will fly above the lingering dark clouds-the rich.

Soaring demand for seats on private jets is likely to continue as the wealthy sidestep many of the barriers facing the masses.

while commercial flights still languish around 40% below pre-pandemic levels.

The huge pent-up demand will help fill planes again on short-haul routes.
